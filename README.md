# Cybersecurity Leadership Report — MoneyGrabbers Insurance Brokerage

A 12-month cybersecurity programme for a fictional 50-person Irish insurance
brokerage handling 450,000 policies annually. Designed as a board-facing
strategy document: risk-led, budget-constrained (€10,000), and explicitly
framed around influence without direct authority.

## The Scenario

MoneyGrabbers is a fictional SME with no existing cybersecurity function:
unpatched SQL Server 2012, unencrypted laptops, BYOD with zero controls,
a single point of failure on email/file infrastructure, and nightly VPN
feeds from insurance partners with no oversight. Typical of the real-world
SME profile that ransomware crews increasingly target.

## What the Report Covers

- **Executive summary** framing cybersecurity as risk transfer, not cost
- **Risk register** with severity ratings and treatment mapped to phases
- **12-month phased roadmap** — immediate risk reduction → governance →
  maturity, with a line-by-line €10k budget allocation
- **Stakeholder engagement strategy** for CEO, Head of IT, CFO, and Ops —
  tailored by what each stakeholder actually values
- **Governance framework** — CIS Controls v8 IG1 in Year 1 as the on-ramp
  to ISO 27001 certification in Year 2
- **Measurable success metrics** for board reporting
- **Hiring case** for a Security Analyst / GRC Coordinator in month 6

## Frameworks & Standards Applied

CIS Controls v8 (IG1) · ISO/IEC 27001:2022 · GDPR (Articles 28, 32, 44) ·
MITRE ATT&CK · ENISA Threat Landscape · FS-ISAC sector intelligence

## Skills Demonstrated

- Risk assessment and register design
- Budget prioritisation under hard constraints
- Executive and board-level communication
- Policy and governance architecture
- Third-party / supply chain risk (insurance partner VPN feeds)
- Cloud migration security (M365, data residency, Article 44)
- BYOD and mobile device strategy
- Culture change and security awareness programme design
